<p><ul><li>Internal Condensing Oil Fired Furnace</li><li>External Condensing Oil Fired Furnace</li><li>Non Condensing Oil Fired Furnace</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Oil Fired Furnaces can be categorized into three main types based on their condensing capabilities. Internal Condensing Oil Fired Furnaces are designed to capture and utilize heat from exhaust gases internally, increasing efficiency. External Condensing Oil Fired Furnaces achieve the same goal but with an external heat exchanger. Non-Condensing Oil Fired Furnaces do not have this feature, making them less efficient in utilizing heat. Each type caters to different needs and budgets, offering varying levels of energy efficiency and heating performance.</p></p>
