<p><p>Loss Prevention strategies in retail typically focus on several market types, including shoplifting, return fraud, employee theft, administrative errors, and other miscellaneous losses. Shoplifting involves customers stealing merchandise, while return fraud involves fraudulent returns of products for cash or store credit. Employee theft occurs when staff members steal from the store, and administrative errors refer to mistakes made in inventory management or accounting. Other market types of losses may include vendor fraud, supply chain errors, or damage to products during transit. Effective loss prevention measures are crucial for minimizing these risks and protecting a retailer's bottom line.</p></p>
