<p><p>Organic iodide contrast agents are commonly used in medical imaging to enhance the visibility of organs, blood vessels, and tissues. There are three main types of organic iodide contrast agents: Ion Type, Non-ionic Type, and Nonionic Dimers. Ion Type agents contain positively charged iodine atoms, while Non-ionic Type agents do not have a charge. Nonionic Dimers are a newer, more stable form of Non-ionic agents that offer improved imaging quality and reduced risk of adverse reactions. Each type is used based on the specific imaging needs and patient characteristics.</p></p>
