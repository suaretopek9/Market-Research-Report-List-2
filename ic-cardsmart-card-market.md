<p><p>IC cards, also known as smart cards, are used in two main market types: contact and contactless. Contact IC cards require physical contact with a card reader for data transfer, providing a more secure method of information transmission. On the other hand, contactless IC cards use radio frequency technology to transfer data wirelessly, making transactions more convenient and efficient. Both types of cards are widely used in various industries such as banking, transportation, and healthcare for payment, access control, and identification purposes.</p></p>
